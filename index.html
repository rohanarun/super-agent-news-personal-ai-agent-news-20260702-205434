The week in personal AI agents

Gemini Spark lands on macOS

Google has shipped Gemini Spark into its macOS app, giving Gemini deeper access to local files, third‑party apps, and real‑time monitoring. It’s an aggressive push to make Gemini a standing personal agent rather than a chat box.

techcrunch.com

Trust & data concerns rise

As Gemini expands into desktop control and remote task execution, questions around data use and permission boundaries are resurfacing — especially when agents can act across devices.

techgenyz.com

Open agents hit security walls

Security researchers reported shell‑injection flaws across most surveyed open‑source AI agents, highlighting how fragile real computer‑use becomes without careful sandboxing.

securityaffairs.com

The competitive landscape

ChatGPT

World‑class conversational AI evolving toward agents and scheduled tasks. Strong for reasoning and one‑off work, less proven for durable computer‑use loops.

Gemini

With Spark and Gemini 3.5 Flash, Google is pushing hard into desktop control, app connections, and real‑time monitoring.

Siri

Deeply embedded, voice‑first assistant. Improvements are coming, but complex computer‑use workflows remain limited.

Grok

Opinionated assistant tied to real‑time and social context. Best understood as a reactive companion rather than an operational agent.

Folk

Primarily a CRM and relationship tool that uses AI within a narrower workflow — not positioned as a general personal agent.

Orchids

Experimental approaches to automation and agents, often focused on research and prototypes rather than production workflows.
